Quick answers, just for teens.

Common questions about our programs

How can I handle school stress better? +
Start with time management - break big tasks into smaller chunks, use a planner, and set realistic goals. Don't forget to take breaks, get enough sleep, and talk to someone when you're feeling overwhelmed. Our mentors can help you create a personalized stress management plan.
I'm worried about my future. What should I do? +
It's totally normal to feel uncertain about the future! Start by exploring your interests through our career guidance program. Try different activities, talk to professionals in fields that interest you, and remember that it's okay to change directions as you learn more about yourself.
How can I make friends and build confidence? +
Join activities that match your interests - it's easier to connect with people who share your passions. Be yourself, practice active listening, and don't be afraid to start conversations. Our social skills workshops and peer support groups can help you build confidence naturally.
What if I'm struggling with mental health? +
You're not alone, and seeking help shows strength, not weakness. We offer confidential counseling services, peer support groups, and wellness workshops. If you're in crisis, reach out to a trusted adult, call a helpline, or contact our emergency support team immediately.
How do I get started with GSI programs? +
Simply fill out our online application or contact us directly. We'll match you with programs that fit your interests and goals. All our teen programs are completely free, and we provide support every step of the way.

About Global Shield Initiative

Protecting communities, empowering youth since 2018

Founded in 2018, Global Shield Initiative emerged from a simple belief: communities facing climate disasters deserve both immediate protection and long-term empowerment. We bridge emergency response with sustainable development, always with young people at the center of our mission.

Today, we operate in 45 countries, protecting vulnerable communities through emergency response, resilient infrastructure, and advocacy while empowering over 2.3 million teens with the skills, confidence, and opportunities they need to become tomorrow's changemakers.
